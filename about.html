The Designer


Anya is fresh UX/IU Designer, who has 3 months of experience doing UI Design for Cinemaloop Inc., and experience doing both UX and UI Design on 3 projects, while studying at UC Berkeley Extension. Her previous background was in film make-up and software QA.

This mixture of creative and technical background help her create compositions with empathy to the specific user, down to the last detail. If there is no answer, she will find it, whatever it takes to get there. She can crank out those side-by-side's, until an answer is found. If time is of the essence, there's music and caffeine.

Anya believes there are no solution-less problems, only a lack of time, or budget. And when it comes to the time-budget issue, she knows how to handle limitations to optimize results.

The Philosophy

Anya wholeheartedly believes in user centric design. This means performing qualitative and quantitative market research to learn who real users are, and identify user needs, so that they can be specifically adressed in Storyboarding the wireframe design, and tested for verification by users each step of the way.

She understands the concept and reasons for implementing Atomic Design, since consistency allows both faster designing, faster development, and faster user recognition. She believes in using UI Grids, to ensure the best placement for legibility, and be kind to the front end developers and save time with consistent spacing. She cares about the Typographic Theory and always keeps in mind the goal, the context, the personality, and how the interaction intended for the user.

Tools of the Trade

No designer would get very far without their tools. Anya's go to design programs are Adobe XD, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, and Sketch.

For project management tools and communication, she has experience with InVision, Trello, Sympli, Workplace, Slack, Skype, Zoom, Scoro, Asana, Jira, and Google Docs, and is familiar with making Ghantt Charts to stay on task.

But she usually starts everything simply with with classics, a TUL pen and paper. And she relies on the magic of sticky notes as well.
