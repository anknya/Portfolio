Paper Prototype #1

I created a paper prototype following the previously discussed process and features of the app with Treehouse Tribe's founder and facilitator. This app would allow the client to initiate a playdate, the neurotypical child's parent to approve or change the time, and the facilitator to then notify them whether she would be able to be present for it.

Check it out

Paper Prototype #2

Upon meeting and reviewing the first prototype, an executive decision was made to make the process more efficient. So I made another paper prototype, which had the facilitator preset their availability, which shortened the scheduling steps from then on.

Check it out

Usability Testing

My team members facilitated some AB testing to understand the ease at which our primary action button is found. The results showed that only 40% of users were able to find the correct button on the first try. so we changed the color of the button and made it bigger.

Future Considerations

Clients had shown interest in a chat box between the client's child and the social peer to practice conversation, and a feature that allows the parent to check in during a playdate, and a paid subscription that enables parents to schedule impromptu playdates with social peers that they have played with previously
