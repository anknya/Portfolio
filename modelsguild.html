Problem Statement

I had discovered that most of the issues of booking manually were regarding efficiency and reliability. And I wondered how I could improve upon this.

Check it out

Storyboard

This storyboard shows how much simpler the process of booking could be with everything automated, except the two steps of bidding on jobs, and accepting jobs.

Check it out

Considerations

I had to consider the values of the models and the organization, as well as their current limitations I wanted to improve upon. So I made my goals be to have all the information go directly from instructor to model, for the eligibility filtering to be done automatically, to allow enough time to think, check, and respond, the ability to unbid easily, and an ability to bid on 2 jobs in one bid, while still allowing many jobs to be booked at once, and keeping the fair aspect (having the backend assign jobs to the model who had booked the least, and imitating a dice roll in case of a tie).

Check it out

Paper Protype

I created a prototype which allows a few days to bid on the jobs. I made it easy to make changes, and gave the ability to check all the details before bidding. I also implemented a bid-on-2 feature, allowing the model to basically state "I'll take this job if I can have that job" making it so if they gets one, they get the other as well, or get neither. This is meant to both be for practicality, to set limits, as well as to preserve the fun aspect from the meetings, by allowing some gambling.

The prototype was demonstrated to Jesse, who found the logic easy to follow, but wished there were instructions when he went to try it himself.
